Vision

A world where all people have freedom to exist in a society that embraces diversity; and individuals develop their potential, live a full life and contribute to society with agency and power.

Mission

TREAT works proactively to inform and creatively transform attitudes, and address injustice and inequalities faced by the trans diverse population in Zimbabwe.

Values

The values TREAT aims to live by are:

  • Courage: TREAT staff and constituency are courageous people, meeting the challenge of living and expressing their trans* identity when possible, at home and in public spaces. They define courage as ‘unafraid to be me, to live my life’ and are working towards making this possible for all trans diverse persons in Zimbabwe.
  • Accountability: in two senses, taking responsibility for resolving issues rather than allowing disagreements to fester, and “The obligation of an individual or organisation to account for its activities, accept responsibility for them, and to disclose the results in a transparent manner. It also includes the responsibility for money or other entrusted property.”
  • Professional: We put aside personal issues and do our jobs well. We keep to the defined standards – are honest and accountable.
  • Confidentiality: We do not gossip. We keep shared confidences private.
  • Humanity/Ubuntu: encourages humanity, creativity and the growth of all, collaborative decision-making and cooperation for the good of all.
  • Resourceful: We are creative in finding resources and using them wisely and honestly in line with the defined purpose
