Organisational Profile

Background

:

TREAT is an organisation founded in Bulawayo, Zimbabwe. It seeks to research and facilitate the provision of empirical evidence as to the needs and qualitative existence of young persons especially those who traditionally face barriers to accessing opportunities and spaces. This in order to facilitate adequate health supports both in general terms and psychosocially, for them. The organisation is not membership based but has 4 members of the secretariat who are responsible for programming and administrative duties and a board of trustees of 6 persons.


Objectives of TREAT


  • To conduct research and collect data on all aspects relating to access to opportunities and spaces for diverse marginalised young persons in the Zimbabwean population.
  • To encourage a culture of mental health and wellness
  • To promote social enterprising to empower young persons on the margins to be productive citizens
  • To network with other stakeholders such as government departments, local government and nongovernmental organisations.
  • To train medical and healthcare professionals on special needs of young persons
  • To encourage young persons in fighting HIV and Aids related problems.
  • To acquire materials and resources to be used in carrying out Trust programs.
  • To educate communities on challenges faced by young persons on the margins.
  • To develop the skills of the secretariat of the TRUST

Who TREAT works with:


TREAT works with various medical practitioners and service providers as well as legal research practitioners in Bulawayo and Matabeleland and occasionally in other parts of the country. We also work with families of young persons on the margins, counselling and psychosocial practitioners, organisations/groups and individuals. We work with independent researchers and other research institutes in order to achieve our organisational objectives.
